How Meridian Works

The Flow Field

A 3D Perlin noise field generates the invisible atmosphere. Layered octaves of fractal noise produce organic, turbulent motion. Every particle type shares this field — rain bends flight trails, wind reshapes everything.

Flight Data

Each tracked aircraft becomes a particle emitter. Streams of light trace air corridors across the canvas.

altitude → color velocity → particle size heading → stream direction

Deep blue at low altitude, through teal, amber, white at cruising height. Attempts live data from OpenSky Network — falls back to simulated corridors that mimic real traffic patterns when CORS blocks the request.

Weather as Medium

Weather shapes the physics of the entire system. Live data from Open-Meteo for Santa Clara feeds directly into the flow field:

wind → flow direction + force pressure → field tightness humidity → trail persistence temperature → color spectrum shift clouds → trail fade + fog precipitation → rain particles + turbulence

High pressure compresses streams into tight ribbons. Low pressure lets them expand and tangle. Rain falls through the same field as flights — where they cross, additive blending makes them bloom. Wind wisps trace faint lines showing the air current. A stormy day and a clear day produce completely different pieces.

Sonification

The same data that drives the visuals generates the audio. Built on Tone.js:

altitude → FM drone pitch wind → filtered brown noise humidity → reverb depth clouds → feedback delay temperature → pad harmonic particle density → harmonic pings

A low FM drone shifts with average flight altitude. Wind becomes shaped noise. Humidity thickens the reverb. Crystalline pings emerge from the particle field. Cold days sound darker. Warm days resolve upward.

Interaction

The cursor acts as a gravitational lens — it bends the flow field perpendicular to itself, pulling all particle types into a swirling vortex. Parallax shift reveals depth as different altitude layers respond at different rates.
